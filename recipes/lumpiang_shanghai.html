Lumpiang Shanghai

Description

Lumpiang Shanghai is a popular Filipino appetizer that consists of ground meat (usually pork) mixed with vegetables and seasonings, wrapped in a thin spring roll wrapper, and deep-fried until crispy. It's a delicious and savory dish that is often served with a sweet and tangy dipping sauce. Lumpiang Shanghai is a favorite at parties and gatherings, and it's sure to be a hit with anyone who loves flavorful and crispy snacks!

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped carrots
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped green onions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 20-25 spring roll wrappers
  • Cooking oil for frying

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, chopped onions, carrots, green onions, minced garlic, soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), salt, and black pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  2. Take a spring roll wrapper and place about 1-2 tablespoons of the pork mixture near one corner of the wrapper. Roll the wrapper tightly around the filling, folding in the sides as you go, until you reach the end. Seal the edge with a little water to ensure it stays closed. Repeat this process with the remaining wrappers and filling.
  3. Heat cooking oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the lumpiang shanghai in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry until they are golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per batch. Use a slotted spoon to remove the fried lumpiang shanghai and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  4. Serve the lumpiang shanghai hot with your favorite dipping sauce, such as sweet chili sauce or a vinegar-based sauce. Enjoy!
