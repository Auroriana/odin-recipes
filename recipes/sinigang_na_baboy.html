Sinigang na Baboy

Description

Sinigang na Baboy is a traditional Filipino soup known for its sour and savory flavor. It is made with pork, tamarind, and a variety of vegetables such as tomatoes, onions, and leafy greens. The tamarind gives the soup its signature tangy taste, while the pork adds a rich and hearty flavor. Sinigang na Baboy is often enjoyed with steamed rice and is a comforting dish that is perfect for rainy days or when you want a warm and satisfying meal. It's a beloved dish in Filipino cuisine and is sure to please anyone who loves flavorful and comforting soups!

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork (preferably pork belly or shoulder), cut into chunks
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 cup tamarind paste or 1/2 cup tamarind concentrate
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 cup daikon radish, sliced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed
  • 1 cup spinach or kangkong (water spinach)
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (patis)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, combine the pork, onion, tomatoes, and water. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the pork is tender.
  2. Remove the pork from the pot and set aside. Strain the broth to remove the solids, then return the broth to the pot.
  3. Stir in the tamarind paste or concentrate, and fish sauce. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Add the daikon radish and green beans to the pot. Simmer for about 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Return the pork to the pot and add the spinach or kangkong. Simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the greens are wilted.
  6. Serve the sinigang na baboy hot with steamed rice. Enjoy!
